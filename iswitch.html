OVERVIEW

As part of the project, I have developed the concept of a fully digital and touchscreen Switchboard as an alternative for the conventional switch boards which have several disadvantages, such as the agony behind memorizing the different switches of different appliances, the time spent in trial and error methods to find out the controls, etc. The final prototype was designed by me after going through an extensive and detailed Design cycle procedure.

Project Methodology

Defining the Problem

Allow me to make you understand the problem better, before I talk about my proposed solution.

Look at the pictures. First is a living room with two ceiling fans. Then you see a conference room with too many lights. We all must have been to places similar to the images shown below. What do you find in common?

Multiple electrical appliances. It’s not tough to guess that there must be a switch board in the room, similar to the one on the left. You must be very well acquainted with these, which are a common sight in classrooms, conference halls, auditoriums, and even living rooms. You must have tried clicking multiple switches by trial and error to get the correct lights or fans turned on. This is really agonizing.

Analysis of Existing Products

As part of this process, I analysed the features of conventional switch boards, its disadvantages; the existing alternatives and their shortcomings as well.

Conventional Switchboard

  • Good for smaller rooms(preferably at home), with less appliances.
  • Not good for rooms meant for public, like classrooms or meeting halls/ auditoriums.
  • Risk of electric shocks
  • Need for remembering switches.
  • Trial and error method required to guess switches for any appliance
  • Require you to call an electrician when there is addition/ removal of appliances and hence requires manipulation of switches.
  • Not time efficient, though cheap

Mobile/Web Smart Home Apps

  • Many apps like Samsung Smart Home, Google Home, Smart Home Solution, Smart Home, etc. already available in the PlayStore.
  • User ratings is not appreciable.
  • As suggested by the names, these are usually for HOME; and can’t be controlled by guests
  • Also, can’t be of any use in public-purpose rooms
  • Good for limited number of appliances
  • Requires ‘Smart Appliances’ (like Smart LED bulbs, etc.) which can be connected to the Apps which implies, extra expenditure.

Remote-controlled Devices

  • Expensive
  • Can’t operate conventional devices,normal fans or lights; which requires you to buy new appliances
  • Different appliances may require different remotes
  • Memorizing different controls in remotes can be hectic
  • Remote may be misplaced
  • Single remote for a large group of people in a big room doesn’t cater to everyone’s needs.

Defining Product Objectives

After my research of the existing products (and its alternatives) and analysis of data collected from the preliminary survey, I was to define why iSWITCH is better than its competitors

  • First in the field to incorporate ceiling’s/room’s graphical layout in the interface
  • Easily visible controls, no need to memorize
  • Touch-sensitive interface
  • Good for home as well as any public-purpose rooms (auditoriums, class rooms)
  • Can incorporate any number of appliances
  • Time efficient
  • No risk of electric shocks
  • Doesn’t require you to buy new devices/ appliances. Works well with Smart or otherwise appliances
  • Can also be controlled by visitors and guests
  • No extra expenditure- one-time installation in place of a conventional switch-board.
  • Shows status of devices (ON/OFF)
  • Can be linked to Apps or remotes

To sum it, the main objective was to develop the concept of a switch board which is easy to use, convenient and cheap.

Prototyping

After having the product objectives clear in mind, I started sketching out the product interface. Then, I discussed those sketches with my Project supervisor, Prof. B. B. V. L. Deepak, in order to get ideas & feedback, while also checking technical feasibility.

Below is the earliest prototype, made using Adobe Photoshop. After that, I conducted another round of Survey to get feedback on the design solution, to uncover more about user goals and pain-points


  • Homescreen of the device shows layout of electrical appliances in the room
  • To better understand directions/ locations of appliances, there's an option to select a reference object on one side, like door/ blackboard/ etc.
  • Appliances can be switched on/off with a click
  • Sliding across the screen can give an isometric view of the room, with other appliances like fridge, oven etc
  • The appliances which are turned on are brighter and the other appliances are dimmed out.

After showing the prototype to some perspective users, belonging to different age groups, I arrived at the following:

  • To include an app, so that the device can be controlled from a distance, or while being away from home. (The device can't always lie within the reach of users, their mobile can, though.)
  • To add a few more (not many, though, which would create clutter) features to make the device interesting
  • To work on aesthetics
  • To add controls like fan speed, light intensity

The App

I also discussed with concerned engineers regarding the technical feasibility of the product. The actualization of the product is now under way, thanks to some generous, intelligent friends of mine.
